// About Quantum Longevity

Engineering a longer, better life.

We are physicians, geneticists, and translational researchers building the kind of longevity practice we ourselves want to be members of: rigorous, restrained, and measured in decades.

// 01 — Why we exist

A clinic built on two questions.

Our mission

Bring the rigor of academic medicine to the intimate scale of a private practice — and measure what matters, for as long as it matters.

We are not satisfied with treating the diseases of aging. We aim to delay, attenuate, and where possible reverse the underlying biological processes that drive them.

Our vision

A world in which a person at 80 walks into their next decade as capable as they were at 50.

Healthspan is the goal. Lifespan is the side effect. Our work is to make those years not just longer, but lived at full capacity — physical, cognitive, and emotional.

// 02 — Founder

A clinic shaped by one stubborn question.

In 2007, after fifteen years splitting time between Stanford's molecular biology labs and a private internal medicine practice in Zurich, Dr. Anya Reinhardt grew frustrated with a peculiar gap. Academic medicine could measure aging at the molecular scale with extraordinary precision; private medicine could deliver care with extraordinary intimacy. The two rarely met.

Quantum Longevity began as a deliberate attempt to close that gap. The first location opened in a converted townhouse on Bahnhofstrasse with three clinicians, one geneticist, and twelve founding members. The thesis was simple: bring the rigor of a research lab to the bedside of a private clinic, and stay with members long enough to actually see what works.

Sixteen years later, the practice serves over ten thousand members across three continents — and our average member relationship is now nine years and counting.

// 04 — Core Values

Four principles, uncompromised.

01

Restraint over performance

We do less, more precisely. The number of interventions is not a measure of quality; the right intervention is.

02

Evidence over enthusiasm

Every protocol references peer-reviewed work. If a therapy lacks evidence, we say so — and then we may study it ourselves.

03

Decades, not visits

Our relationships are measured in years. We follow members across major life transitions, not single appointments.

04

Privacy as architecture

Member data is encrypted at rest and in transit, segregated by access scope, and never sold, licensed, or shared.
